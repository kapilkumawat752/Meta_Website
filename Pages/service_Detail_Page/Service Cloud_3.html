Service Cloud- Your companion for managing customer service.


Service Cloud is another vertical of Salesforce which deals in customer service management. Once a product or service is sold, it becomes evident to provide industry-leading customer service. However, at times with the number of incoming calls, or emails or texts, it can be a tedious task to manage everything on time. This is where Service Cloud comes into play. With Service Cloud, you can organize and store all the incoming information in one place. This helps in providing better, faster and efficient customer support or service, where an agent can help in solving the problems.

Why service cloud

The very first reason is its availability. Being cloud-based, you can access, monitor, and provide customer service, practically from anywhere. Salesforce always comes in with the benefit of customizing and the same can be done with Service Cloud. It can be customized as per your business requirements. Another important aspect of Service Cloud is that it is industry-independent, so any industry can turn their customer service experience 360 degrees (obviously in a positive direction) with the help of Service Cloud. There are two main objects where all the information is stored, be it related to issues reported by the customer or be it the solution of any problem.

  • Cases - Whenever a customer has to report an issue or wants to enquire any feature, that information is stored in the Case object. It can be integrated with all the channels from where a customer can reach your business. All that information is then stored in this object, creating a repository to store everything in one place, just like what Leads objects do in Sales Cloud.
  • Solutions - Every problem has a verified solution, which can then be saved in the Solutions object. With this object, you can create a knowledge base effortlessly for the solution to common problems.

With these objects, you can not only improve your customer satisfaction level but can also impact an agent's experience. With everything being streamlined, a plethora of knowledge base, response time can be significantly reduced, improving the agents' experience. All the incoming issues or feedbacks are being routed, through omnichannel, to the best-suited agent based on their skillset and availability. It is not just limited to those objects. The data collected in those objects have their importance.

  • Entitlements - This usually covers as to what all types of services you are entitled to provide to your customers, irrespective of the product or services they have purchased.
  • Milestones - It gives a brief overview as to what all parts of the services are being provided and what all are remaining.
  • Service Legal Agreement(SLA) - This mentions the terms and conditions of the services being provided along with the desired turnaround time.
  • Chatter - A complete overview of all the communications done with the customer using the service console. This console can again be customized as per your need and requirements, to make the job easy for your customer support agent.
  • Live agents - Bots are taking over the initial customer interaction. Embedding with the Solutions object can make these bots perform like actual agents providing readymade solutions to the customers.
  • Telephonic support - Using the third - party apps and getting them integrated with the Service Cloud platform can help you in the running your call center more professionally.
  • Dashboard - Ease of access to daily reports can help you in getting numbers. Be it related to average calls per day or the average resolution time, everything can be monitored through graphs. You will notice a drastic change in the reports after implementing Service Cloud for your customer service management.
